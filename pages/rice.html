Rice

how to cook rice

Ingredients

  • 1 cup white rice (long-grain, jasmine, or basmati)
  • 1 ¬Ω cups water (some methods use 2 cups, but 1.5 cups often results in fluffier rice)
  • A pinch of salt (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon butter or oil (optional, helps prevent sticking and adds flavor)

Instructions

  • Rinse the rice (optional but recommended): For fluffier, individual grains, rinse the rice under cold running water in a fine-mesh strainer until the water runs clear. This removes excess starch.
  • Combine ingredients: Place the rinsed (or unrinsed) rice, water, and salt (and butter/oil, if using) in a medium saucepan with a tight-fitting lid.
  • Bring to a boil: Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat with the lid off. Stir once to combine everything.
  • Reduce heat and simmer: Once boiling, immediately reduce the heat to the lowest setting, cover the pot tightly with the lid, and let it simmer for about 15 minutes. Do not lift the lid while it simmers.
  • Rest (steam): Remove the saucepan from the heat entirely, but leave the lid on. Let the rice rest, covered, for another 5-10 minutes. This allows the steam to finish cooking the grains and ensures the moisture is evenly distributed.
  • Fluff and serve: Finally, remove the lid and gently fluff the rice with a fork or rice paddle.

Tips

  • Water Ratio: A general rule is 1 part rice to 1.5-2 parts water.
  • Lid On: Keeping the lid on throughout the simmering and resting process is crucial for trapping the steam needed to cook the rice properly.
  • Don't Stir: Avoid stirring after the initial mix, as this can break the grains and make the rice mushy.
